📘 NSE Swing Scanner: User Guide
The NSE Swing Scanner is a technical decision-support tool designed to identify stocks that are transitioning from a consolidation phase to a bullish trend.
1. How to Use the App
A. Individual Analysis (Deep Dive)
If you have a specific stock in mind (e.g., TATASTEEL), use this tab to get an instant "Go/No-Go" decision.
Enter the symbol (without .NS).
Review the Swing Score:
80-100: High probability setup.
60-79: Potential setup, but requires "confirmation" (like a price breakout).
Below 60: Weak structure; avoid for now.
Check the Trade Setup box for your exact Entry, Stop Loss, and Targets.
B. Daily Market Scanner (Discovery)
Use this every day after 4:00 PM IST (once market data settles).
Click "Run Automated Scan."
The system will process the watchlist and rank stocks from highest to lowest score.
Expand the results to see the "Reasons" — look for stocks that have multiple checkmarks (e.g., "Price > EMA200" + "High Volume").
2. Selection Criteria: How to Pick a Winning Swing Trade
The app uses a "Point System" to calculate the score. Here is the criteria you should look for when selecting a stock from the scanner:
✅ Criterion 1: The "Institutional" Trend (EMA 200)
The Logic: Large banks and institutions usually buy stocks trading above the 200-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA).
Action: Only prioritize stocks where the analysis says "Price above EMA200." Trading below this is considered "bottom fishing," which is risky for swing trading.
✅ Criterion 2: The "Voluntary" Momentum (RSI & MACD)
The Logic: We want stocks that are moving, not sitting still.
The Sweet Spot: An RSI between 50 and 70 is perfect. If the RSI is above 75, the stock is "Overbought" (too hot to handle). If it's below 40, it's too weak.
MACD: A positive MACD histogram indicates that the bullish momentum is increasing.
✅ Criterion 3: The "Big Money" Footprint (Volume)
The Logic: This is the most important indicator. High volume means "Smart Money" is entering.
Action: Look for the reason "High Volume Breakout." This means today's volume is at least 50% higher than the last 20 days. This is a high-conviction signal.
3. Trade Execution Rules (The 3 Golden Rules)
Even with a 90/100 score, follow these rules to protect your capital:
Don't Chase the Gap: If a stock shows up on the scanner but it has already jumped 5% or more in a single day, wait for a small 1-2 day "pullback" before entering.
The Stop Loss is Non-Negotiable: The app calculates the Stop Loss based on the EMA 50 or a recent low. If the price hits this level, exit immediately. No exceptions.
The 2:1 Target Strategy:
Target 1: When the price hits Target 1, sell 50% of your position and move your Stop Loss to your Entry Price. This ensures a "Risk-Free" trade.
Target 2: Hold the remaining 50% for the bigger move.
4. When to AVOID Trading
Even if the app gives a high score, do not take the trade if:
Market Sentiment is Bearish: If the Nifty 50 or Sensex is down more than 1.5% for the day, most swing trades will fail regardless of individual stock strength.
Earnings Release: If the company is announcing quarterly results in the next 2-3 days, avoid the trade. Earnings create "gap" risks that technical analysis cannot predict.
